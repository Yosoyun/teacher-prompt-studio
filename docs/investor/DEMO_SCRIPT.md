# Teacher Prompt Studio — Investor Demo Script

- **Length:** 5 minutes
- **Audience:** investor, accelerator partner or school/coaching pilot buyer
- **Scenario:** Class 10 CBSE mathematics assessment on quadratic equations

## Demo truth rules

1. Say explicitly that an external AI generates the final artifact today.
2. Do not claim Teacher Prompt Studio independently validated a file; that is roadmap.
3. Distinguish a teacher-confirmed “usable” rating from an audited quality pass.
4. Do not paste learner data, school-private material or unsupported current board rules.
5. If the live provider fails, show the failure and use a clearly labelled pre-generated example. Never pretend a recording is live.

## Before the meeting

- Open Teacher Prompt Studio and one supported AI account.
- Prepare a fresh browser profile or clear the local demo ledger.
- Have one anonymised provider-generated example available as a fallback, labelled with provider and generation date.
- Keep the official market-context source ready: [Ministry of Education UDISE+ 2025–26 release, 7 July 2026](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2282141&lang=1&reg=48).
- Do not use Harvard, MIT, school or board logos as implied endorsements.

## 0:00–0:30 — problem

**Say**

> Teachers do not need better prompts. They need a question paper they can actually print: correct totals, separate student and teacher files, readable Hindi, complete answers and no invented board claims. Generic chat makes the teacher specify and inspect all of that manually.

**Show**

- The Teacher Prompt Studio home and the four-step rail.
- The “prompt stays backstage” positioning.

## 0:30–1:20 — choose the job

**Do**

1. Select **Question paper**.
2. Point out that the workflow automatically loads its artifact contract and recommended controls.
3. Avoid opening the full 79-workflow library unless asked.

**Say**

> We are not pitching 79 prompts. Our wedge is repeated assessment production for Indian secondary and coaching teachers. The wider library is an expansion surface.

## 1:20–2:05 — fit the classroom

**Do**

- Board: **CBSE / NCERT**
- Class: **10**
- Subject: **Mathematics**
- Topic: **Quadratic equations**
- Language: **Hindi + English**
- Keep class size visible to show classroom constraints.

**Say**

> The teacher makes classroom decisions, not prompt-engineering decisions. Exact current paper rules still require teacher-supplied authoritative material; the product refuses to invent them.

## 2:05–2:55 — define the real artifact

**Do**

1. Choose the **PDF + DOCX bundle**.
2. Select the assessment architecture and question volume.
3. Show the manifest: student PDF, editable student DOCX and teacher assessment pack.
4. Briefly show the file firewall, item/mark totals, multilingual and release requirements.

**Say**

> This is the core product asset: a structured production contract. The model is told which physical files must exist, what each may contain, and what causes release failure.

## 2:55–3:40 — launch the external AI

**Do**

1. Enter the final Launch AI step.
2. Point out ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini and the other provider choices.
3. Click one provider; the studio copies the current build instructions and opens a fresh external chat.
4. Paste once. Do not hide the provider boundary.

**Say**

> Today, Teacher Prompt Studio compiles and hands off the job. This external AI generates the files. File capability varies by provider, model and plan. We are building the provider-agnostic layer above that variability.

If generation will take longer than the meeting, switch to the labelled fallback artifact and say so.

## 3:40–4:20 — close the outcome loop

**Do**

1. Return to Teacher Prompt Studio.
2. Show the three honest outcome choices: usable file, needs repair, or text only.
3. Choose the outcome that actually occurred.
4. If repair is needed, copy the same-chat repair instruction.
5. Open **My impact** and show that the setup can be reused.

**Say**

> This is device-local teacher evidence, not global traction and not independent validation. It preserves trust while we test whether teachers repeatedly get usable artifacts.

## 4:20–4:50 — show the moat roadmap

**Say**

> The next product milestone is to bring the returned files back, verify manifests, totals, placeholders, teacher-answer leakage, fonts and rendering independently, then learn which workflow and provider succeeds for each board, subject, class and language. That workflow-quality graph—not a prompt library—is the moat.

**Show**

- The delivery receipt and quality requirements.
- The device-local history and reuse action.
- A simple before/after repair artifact if permission allows.

## 4:50–5:00 — close

**Say**

> We are starting with Indian secondary and coaching assessment production. In 12 weeks we aim to prove repeat use, usable-file quality and paid continuation before expanding the product or the market story.

## Questions likely to follow

### “Why will ChatGPT not build this?”

**Answer:** It may improve generic setup and file creation. Our bet is that teachers and institutions still need persistent workflow context, deterministic artifact checks, provider routing, approved template lineage and governance across models. This remains a hypothesis until retention and comparative quality data prove it.

### “What is your traction?”

**Answer:** No verified company-wide user, revenue or retention figures have been supplied. The current browser-local ledger is designed to start collecting teacher-confirmed pilot evidence without misrepresenting it as global analytics.

### “How large is the market?”

**Answer:** The Ministry of Education's UDISE+ 2025–26 release reports 10,273,020 teachers across Indian school education, compared with 10,122,420 in 2024–25. That is context, not our TAM. We are first measuring the smaller segment of secondary/coaching teachers with frequent assessment-production needs and AI access.

### “Who pays?”

**Answer:** **[HYPOTHESIS]** Individual teachers may pay for verification and reuse; departments and coaching teams may pay for shared approved templates and governance. The pilot target is real paid continuation from teachers or organisational buyers.

### “What is proprietary today?”

**Answer:** The structured compiler, artifact schemas, preflight rules and workflow library are shipped assets, but not a durable moat alone. Independent validation, provider evaluation data, repair outcomes and approved template lineage are roadmap assets.

### “What are you raising?”

**Answer:** **[UNKNOWN — founders must supply amount, runway, milestones and use of funds].**

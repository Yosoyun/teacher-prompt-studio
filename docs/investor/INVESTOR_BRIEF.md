- **Product:** [Teacher Prompt Studio](https://yosoyun.github.io/teacher-prompt-studio/)
- **Wedge:** Indian secondary and coaching assessment production

## The one-line pitch

Teacher Prompt Studio turns a few classroom decisions into a rigorous production brief for question papers, DPPs and teaching packs, then hands that brief to the teacher's chosen AI with Indian board, language, file and quality requirements already built in.

**Current boundary:** final artifacts are generated by an external AI provider. The product prepares the instructions, opens the provider and records a teacher-confirmed result; it does not yet independently inspect the returned files.

## Problem

Teachers need finished classroom materials, not lessons in prompt engineering. A generic AI chat can return plausible text while still failing the real job: wrong item totals, incomplete questions, mixed teacher/student content, broken Indic glyphs, invented board claims, missing answer files or an unusable layout.

**[HYPOTHESIS]** This pain is sharpest and most frequent among secondary-school and coaching teachers who repeatedly create assessments and practice material under time pressure.

## Initial customer

**Primary ICP hypothesis**

- Subject teachers and coaching faculty serving Classes 6–12 in India.
- Initially mathematics and science assessment-heavy workflows.
- Repeated jobs: question papers, DPPs, worked solutions, theory notes and chapter packs.
- Existing access to at least one consumer AI provider.
- Works in a school department, tuition centre or coaching team where a useful template can spread to colleagues.

This is deliberately narrower than “all teachers.” Early-years, university, professional learning and the broader workflow library are expansion surfaces.

The impact ledger is self-reported evidence, not independent artifact validation and not global product analytics.

## Why this can become a company

The product should not compete as a larger prompt library. Its opportunity is to own the workflow between a teacher's intent and a classroom-safe artifact:

1. Capture the classroom and assessment contract.
2. Route the job to a capable provider.
3. Receive or import the resulting files.
4. Check manifests, totals, placeholders, audience leakage, fonts and rendering.
5. Repair failures without losing context.
6. Save the approved setup for the next chapter, class or colleague.

Steps 1–2 and teacher-confirmed outcome/reuse are shipped. Independent receipt and verification of generated files are roadmap items.

## Go-to-market hypotheses

1. **Artifact-led teacher acquisition:** demonstrate a finished question paper or DPP in teacher YouTube, WhatsApp and Telegram communities; lead with the artifact, not the hidden prompt.
2. **Reuse and referral:** let a teacher repeat a successful setup and share a non-sensitive configuration with a colleague.
3. **Teacher-creator collections:** invite credible teachers to maintain versioned, reviewed recipe collections.
4. **Bottom-up institution path:** individual teacher → department/coaching team → paid shared library and governance → school or chain rollout.

SEO and broad feature discovery may support acquisition, but they are not yet proven distribution channels.

## Retention loop

Choose mission → generate externally → confirm outcome → repair in the same AI chat → mark usable → reuse the setup → share or approve it for a team.

**[SHIPPED]** The device-local ledger supports confirmation and reuse.

**[ROADMAP]** Cross-device history, shared team templates, independent file verification and institutional approval.

## Business model hypotheses

- **Teacher Pro:** saved/versioned blueprints, independent artifact checks, premium managed production and deeper reuse.
- **Department:** shared approved templates, review workflow, quality evidence and team library.
- **Institution or coaching chain:** provider policy, private source/template library, administration, SSO and support.

Pricing is **[UNKNOWN]**. The next evidence should be real payment or a deposit, not stated willingness in a survey. Because teachers currently use their own external AI, the product should charge for workflow memory, verification, collaboration and governance—not merely for access to prompts.

## Moat roadmap

The current compiler, schemas and preflight rules are useful product assets but are copyable. The defensible layer is a consented, privacy-preserving workflow-quality graph:

- board, class, subject, language and constraint metadata;
- workflow and artifact-contract version;
- provider/model/date capability result;
- deterministic failure labels such as missing file, wrong total, placeholder, glyph, audience leak or render failure;
- repair sequence and attempts to acceptance;
- teacher-confirmed use and independently reviewed quality;
- institution-approved template lineage.

This can improve defaults, routing and verification without storing student work or source content. A repeatable provider evaluation harness and independent artifact validator are the critical technical moat milestones.

## Twelve-week proof plan

The detailed measurement contract is in [PILOT_SCORECARD.md](./PILOT_SCORECARD.md).

- **[TARGET]** Recruit 30 consented teachers across at least 3 departments or coaching teams.
- **[TARGET]** At least 70% activate by completing and rating a first handoff within 7 days.
- **[TARGET]** At least 70% teacher-confirmed usable-file rate among rated handoffs.
- **[TARGET]** At least 40% week-4 and 30% week-12 creator retention among activated teachers.
- **[TARGET]** Manually and independently audit at least 30 anonymised artifact bundles; at least 80% should avoid a major release failure.
- **[TARGET]** Obtain real paid continuation from at least 5 individual teachers or 2 organisational buyers.

All figures above are targets. None are current traction claims.

## Principal risks

| Risk | Current response | Evidence still needed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Generic AI products absorb prompt setup | Own artifact contracts, verification, reuse and institutional workflow | Comparative completion and retention data |
| External providers return inconsistent formats | Provider-specific handoff and repair instructions | Dated, repeatable compatibility tests |
| Prompted checks are mistaken for verified checks | Explicitly disclose that independent validation is roadmap | File-import validator and audited pass/fail evidence |
| Board rules change or are invented | Require teacher-supplied authoritative material for exact alignment | Versioned source governance |
| Teacher/student privacy | Device-local state and anonymisation guidance | Pilot consent, deletion and incident process |
| Output ownership and provenance are unclear to an institution | Metadata provenance is compiled into current build briefs | A transparent, configurable attribution and ownership policy |
| Price sensitivity and procurement cycles | Teacher-first, department-led entry | Paid continuation and renewal evidence |
| Broad feature set dilutes the wedge | Lead with assessment production | Cohort data by workflow and ICP |

# Teacher Prompt Studio — 12-Week Pilot Scorecard

## Purpose

Test whether a narrow group of Indian secondary/coaching teachers repeatedly obtains usable assessment artifacts and will pay to continue. This scorecard is a measurement contract, not a forecast or current traction report.

## Status legend

- **[BASELINE UNKNOWN]** No verified starting value has been supplied.
- **[TARGET]** The threshold the pilot aims to reach by week 12.
- **Teacher-confirmed** means a participant rated the output; it is not independent validation.
- **Independently reviewed** means an anonymised artifact was checked against a written rubric by a reviewer other than the generating model.

## Pilot cohort hypothesis

- **[TARGET]** 30 consented teacher participants.
- **[TARGET]** At least 3 distinct school departments, tuition centres or coaching teams.
- Classes 6–12, initially mathematics and science.
- Primary workflows: question paper, DPP, worked solutions, theory notes and chapter pack.
- Participants already have access to at least one supported external AI.

The cohort design is a hypothesis. Record role, organisation type and workflow frequency so results can be segmented instead of blended.

## North-star outcome

**Weekly teachers who produce and rate at least one artifact as usable.**

This combines return behaviour with a classroom outcome. “Prompt copied,” “AI opened” and site visits are supporting funnel events, not the north star.

## Major release failure rubric

An independently reviewed artifact fails if any of these are present:

1. A required file is missing or the provider returns ordinary chat text only.
2. Question, item or mark totals do not reconcile.
3. An incomplete stem, dummy option, placeholder or unsupported answer remains.
4. Teacher answers, blueprint or marking guidance leak into the student file.
5. Required language text contains broken glyphs or is not readable/searchable.
6. Pages, tables, questions or response areas are clipped or unusable.
7. The artifact claims official board alignment that is not supported by supplied material.

The current product instructs the external AI to perform many of these checks. During this pilot, independent review remains manual until a validator is shipped.

## Weekly operating cadence

### Week 0 — prepare

- Freeze the five pilot workflows and written review rubric.
- Obtain consent and define the no-learner-data rule.
- Create participant IDs that do not expose student information.
- Record each participant's current process and time for one comparable job.

### Weeks 1–2 — activation

- Observe first builds without taking control of the workflow.
- Record time to handoff, provider, failure mode and outcome rating.
- Interview every non-activating participant.

### Weeks 3–4 — product fit

- Review the first cohort of anonymised artifacts.
- Fix the highest-frequency failure, not the loudest feature request.
- Calculate week-4 retention by original cohort.

### Weeks 5–8 — repetition

- Test repeat setup use across a new chapter or class.
- Compare the five workflows separately.
- Test one colleague-share path and one department workflow.

### Weeks 9–12 — payment and expansion

- Offer a specific paid continuation tied to verification, reuse or team value.
- Collect money or a deposit; do not count verbal interest as conversion.
- Calculate week-12 retention, audited quality and pilot decision.

## Data collection rules

- Source material, learner responses and full prompts are excluded from the scorecard.
- The browser ledger remains local unless a participant explicitly copies and shares its anonymised summary.
- A teacher-confirmed rating must never be described as independent validation.
- Every aggregate must state numerator, denominator, date range and missing-data count.
- Segment results by workflow, provider and organisation type before combining them.
- Preserve failures and drop-offs; do not report only successful handoffs.

## Pilot decision at week 12

### Continue and narrow

Choose this if one workflow/ICP segment shows repeat use, usable artifacts and paid continuation even when the overall averages are mixed.

### Continue and repair

Choose this if activation is strong but major artifact failures or text-only returns prevent retention. Prioritise the independent validator and provider routing.

### Pause or change wedge

Choose this if teachers do not repeat the workflow, cannot identify material value or will not pay after receiving usable artifacts.

The decision memo must report actual results beside the targets above. Until then, every result remains **[UNKNOWN]**.
